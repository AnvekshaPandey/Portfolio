Urbana Road Safety Intelligence App

Python · Altair · Streamlit · Pandas · Hugging Face

"Urbana is a small city, and as someone who moved here for graduate school, I wanted to understand it better. When I found 10 years of traffic stop data sitting on a public government portal, I saw a chance to turn raw records into something genuinely useful for the community."

10 yrs

City Data Analyzed

4

An interactive analytics dashboard built on 10 years of Urbana police data, uncovering when and where traffic stops concentrate, which age groups are most affected, how violation types differ across demographics, and how crime categories have shifted over time.

PythonAltairStreamlitPandasHugging FaceData Cleaning

Sourced raw data directly from the Illinois Open Data Portal (City of Urbana / IDOT Traffic Stop dataset), a real government dataset with 10+ years of police stop records covering demographics, violation types, time of day, and outcomes.

Resolved messy real-world data challenges: mixed date formats, invalid age fields converted to NaN, and hundreds of granular crime descriptions collapsed into three clean categories using keyword-based classification.

Engineered 4 linked Altair visualizations wired to year/month filter sliders: Age vs. Violation Type line chart, Traffic Stop Heatmap (hour × day of week), Stacked Area Chart by race over time, and a Crime Category Trends line chart.

Key findings: Stops peak mid-morning on weekdays (9–12 PM), drivers aged 20–29 dominate every violation type, drug-related incidents declined sharply after 2018, and violent crime trended upward through 2022.

Deployed publicly as an interactive Streamlit app on Hugging Face Spaces, anveksha-trafficsignalchart.hf.space

F1 Podium Predictor

Python · XGBoost · Scikit-learn · Feature Engineering · Hugging Face

"Every F1 fan has an opinion on who will podium. I wanted to know if 7 seasons of race data, grid position, recovery ability, circuit overtaking rates, could actually do better than a guess."

125+

Races in Training Set

0.663

Final F1 Score

Live

HF Prediction UI

An end-to-end machine learning pipeline that predicts whether an F1 driver will finish on the podium, trained on 7 seasons of race, qualifying, and standings data. Features a hybrid scoring strategy combining XGBoost probabilities with circuit-specific overtaking weights.

PythonXGBoostScikit-learnTime-Series CVFeature EngineeringREST APIHugging Face

Sourced 7 seasons of race results, qualifying data, and championship standings from the Ergast API Mirror, split strictly by time to prevent leakage: train 2018–2023, validate 2024, test 2025.

Engineered leakage-free features: driver recovery ability, grid penalty factor, championship gap between drivers, and circuit overtaking difficulty — all derived purely from historical records.

Selected XGBoost over Logistic Regression. Addressed ~15% class imbalance via scale_pos_weight, lifting F1 from 0.576 to 0.663 (+8.7%).

Built a hybrid scoring strategy combining ML probability with circuit-specific grid weights tuned via grid search on validation data.

Top features by ablation study: recovery_weighted (largest single impact), grid_penalty_factor, championship_gap — confirming race context beyond raw grid position carries real predictive signal.

Deployed an interactive Hugging Face UI for live race weekend predictions at huggingface.co/spaces/Anveksha/f1-race-predictor
